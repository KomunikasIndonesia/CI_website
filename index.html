At a Glance

How a simple SMS text is the key to farmers' success.

Current State

After graduating from Hutan Biru's sustainability program, the farmers return to their home villages, spread across the island of South Sulawesi. The lack of technology, combined with lack of internet access and distance between villages, impedes farmers' ability to share data, implement a production calendar, or interact with their fellow graduates (now members of the HB Network) outside of their home village. Thus, in itâ€™s current state, membership within the HB Network provides virtually no benefits and members, and they remain unable to coordinate or scale their production, share resources, leverage collective bargaining power, or learn from the successes of their peers.

The farmers in the HB Network do not have acess to internet, but they do have SMS capable phones, so we have developed an innovative solution that uses only the basic cell phones they already posses. By parsing SMS texts, the cloud based app instantly connects the farmers to life changing technology.

Future State

Cloud

The cloud based app allows farmers to log, store, own and selectively share information. It empowers the farmers to track crop yields and prices, set seasonal harvest goals, share successful farming strategies, receive weather updates, and communicate with each other as well as their customers whether they be individuals, middle men, or private companies.

Data Ownership

Because they can grant various levels of viewing and editing access to different users, the farmers can share their data selectively. By empowering the farmers to control who accesses their data, the KI Application transforms data into an asset that can be sold entirely separate from the physical products that the farmers produce.

Impact Monitoring

In addition, this data control also opens the door to efficient, comprehensive impact monitoring. Farmers can grant NGOs and/or government officials the ability to view their data to satisfy their reporting needs.

Timeline

  • June-August 2014

    Diagnosing the Problem

    Kat, the founder, interned at an NGO in South Sulawesi which was working to help local farmers. Once there, Kat was tasked with answering the overwhelmingly broad question of how can we (the NGO) improve? She spent the majority of her summer asking the farmers what they needed to succeed. After numerous interviews, trips to the NGO's village sites, and administering surveys, it became clear that the biggest obstacle the farmers faced in their daily lives was a massive communication gap. At the end of her internship Kat presented her proposed solution to the farmers.

  • August-May 2014-15

    Research & Development

    Once back at Cornell, Kat seeked out the help of faculty advisors and recruited 3 teammates. Equipped with experienced advisors and an interdisciplinary team comprised of a biologist, 2 engineers, and a development sociologist, Kat and the team spent months researching how to best use farmers' cell phones to bridge the communication gap. Detailed plans for a robust, cloud based data sharing app were developed.

  • March 2015

    Clinton Global Initiative Award

    Each year, CGI University hosts a conference where approximately 1,200 students, youth organization leaders, topic experts, and celebrities come together to discuss and develop innovative solutions to pressing global challenges. This year, Communicate Indonesia was named a winner of the CGIU Social Venture Challenge 2015 Award at the conference in Miami.

  • April 2015

    Engaged Cornell Award

    As part of the work by the Engaged Learning and Reserach Intitiative at Cornell University, every year multiple student groups are invited to present philanthropic work completed both on campus and around the globe. At this year's project showcase, Communicate Indonesia received the CGIU Commitment Maker Challenge Award

  • Ongoing

    Product Development

    The team was working with a software engineer at Twillio to together build our cloud based app. Current features include a production calender and access to data spreadsheets using the Twillio API to read SMS texts.

  • Next Steps 2016+

    Needs Assessment

    As soon as we gain enough funding to do so, a baseline survey will be conducted at one of the village sites to understand the farmersâ€™ current agricultural knowledge and technological resources available. The survey will gather information on the farmersâ€™ inputs, communication types/frequencies/ preferences, agricultural outputs, and revenue. The application will then be updated based on the results of the survey.

    App Training and Implementation

    As soon as we have the funding to send her, Kat will return to Indonesia to work directly with the farmers in South Sulawesi and help train them to use the app. As farmers begin using the application, the CI team in collaboration with our partner NGO will administer a launch survey to collect feedback about the functionality of the app. This will allow the team to make sure that the app is designed with functionalities that are farmer-centered.

    Impact Assessment

    In order to assess progress, the CI team and our partner NGO will conduct a post-production survey after one full harvest cycle has taken place. This will allow us to gather data on the impact of our app.

  • Future 2020+

    Expansion

    Once the application has been polished, we will launch the app in other sites. The application will periodically be updated as needed based on feedback from the farmers.
